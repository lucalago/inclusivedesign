What is inclusive design?

Inclusive design is a design methodology that enables and draws on the full range of human diversity. This means including and learning from people with a range of perspectives so that everyone can access, use and enjoy a product or service.

The importance of inclusivity.

This principle is about inviting a diversity of people with a broad range of needs, preferences, interests and skills into the design process without relegating people with disabilities to the role of subjects of research or token participants in design exercises.

Institutions like Microsoft or Cambridge are already trying to encourage people to implement Inclusive Design principles in their work.

Evolution of inclusive design.

Inclusive design is a design methodology that is progressively raising awareness in the field of design whether it be architecture, product design or digital design - since 1973.

We looked for the searches of the keyword “inclusivity” and “inclusive design” and there’s an increase in the number of searches, which suggest that it’s a topic that has become more visible with the decades.

Even though inclusive design is a concept that has been studied for decades, there's still a vast number of designers that misunderstand the concept and don't apply it in the works.

The limited adoption of inclusive design in design practice suggests that there are still many challenges to address, relating to the perceptions of inclusive design.

We developed a framework to assess the participants of this user testing. We asked them beforehand to send us a design project they’ve worked recently and we would ask them a series of questions related to inclusivity.

Some findings from our interviews.

Knowledge

60% of the designers interviewed knew about inclusive design, but any of them used this approach.

Research

68% of the interviewee did some sort of research in the project assessed, but just 25% did interviews with potential users.

Accessibility

The main accessibility elements they included were based on design conventions, but with a lack of validation.

Reasons why they didn’t
design for inclusivity.

Clients

The vast majority of the clients don’t know about this approach, therefore they don’t invest in inclusivity.

Money

Many interviewees mentioned that they didn’t had a suitable budget for research, therefore they couldn’t invest time on it.

Time

When working in an agency you have limited time to work on a client, so many steps of the design process are skipped.
